About Video Game Data

The data used in this website are all found in the CORGIS dataset project, and is found here. The data found in this list ranges from 2004 to 2008. The information on the dataset was gathered from crowd-sourced data on "How Long to Beat".

A statistic that was mentioned in the data was the length of certain playstyles. These numbers were measured in hours, and mean the following:

  • Anything with All Playstyles: Any way to complete the game. Ranges from getting the least possible things to complete the game, to the most possible things to complete the game.
  • Anything with Completionists: Doing everything possible in the game.
  • Anything with Main + Extras: Doing everything that is the main and major extra parts of the game.
  • Anything with Main Story: Completing the main story.
  • Average: the average amount of time that it took for people to finish the game.
  • Leisure: The slowest time that it took someone to finish the game.
  • Median: The median time that it took someone to finish the game.
  • Rushed: The fastest time someone took to finish the game.
  • Polled: How many people responded to the form for each section.

    • Another one of the statistics that is listed is whether a game is licensed or not. If a game is licensed, it means that it is based off a previous licensed game/other thing. Basically, they use assets and other things from another entity.
